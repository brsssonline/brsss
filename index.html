Our Journey
History & Milestones
From a newly inaugurated school in 2005 to a recognised institution of academic excellence — the journey of BRSSS is one of vision, growth and community pride.
2000–2002
Foundation Stone Laid
The foundation stone of the school was laid by Sir Anerood Jugnauth, then Prime Minister of Mauritius. Construction of the school campus in Mapou, Rivière du Rempart District, commenced.
2003
First Students Enrolled
The first cohort of Form I (Grade 7) students were enrolled, beginning the school's academic journey even before its official inauguration.
27 May 2005
Official Inauguration
Mapou State Secondary School was officially inaugurated by Raouf Bundhun, then Vice-President of the Republic of Mauritius, marking the school's formal establishment as a government institution.
14 March 2006
🏛️ Renamed in Honour of Beekrumsing Ramlallah
The school was renamed Shri Beekrumsing Ramlallah State Secondary School in a ceremony presided over by Dr Navin Ramgoolam, then Prime Minister. The renaming honoured the legacy of Beekrumsing Ramlallah — teacher, journalist, politician and social reformer (1915–2000).
2008
🥇 National Excellence Award
BRSSS was ranked as the best school in northern Mauritius and achieved first place nationally in Design & Technology at the Cambridge School Certificate examinations — a landmark moment for the school and the Mapou community.
2010
Digital Presence Established
The school launched its official website at brsss.net, establishing its digital identity and providing a platform for school news, announcements and community engagement.
Early 2019
🌍 Cambridge Top 500 Achievement
For the first time in BRSSS history, a student was ranked in the Cambridge Top 500 — the prestigious global list of highest-achieving Cambridge International Examination candidates. A proud milestone for the school, its students, staff and the wider community.

Our Namesake
Shri Beekrumsing
Ramlallah

"Knowledge gives humility, from humility comes worthiness… from worthiness comes wealth, from wealth comes dharma, and from dharma comes happiness."
— A guiding principle of Beekrumsing Ramlallah

📜 Origins

Born in Long Mountain, British Mauritius in 1915. His great-grandfather migrated from Ballia, northern India, as an indentured labourer. He began his career as a school teacher and social worker.

📰 Mauritius Times (1954)

In 1954, Ramlallah co-founded the Mauritius Times with Sir Kher Jagatsingh. He served as founder and editor-in-chief for 46 years until his death in 2000. The paper championed justice, education and press freedom.

🏛️ Political Career

Elected to the Legislative Council and Assembly in 1959, 1963 and 1967, representing Constituency No. 6 (Grand Baie–Poudre D'Or). Served as Parliamentary Secretary and Minister until 1976.

🏛️ Aapravasi Ghat

Championed the preservation of the historic landing site of indentured labourers — the Coolie Ghat — now a UNESCO World Heritage Site known as Aapravasi Ghat. He began remembrance ceremonies in 1970.

⚖️ Press Freedom Hero

In 1984, as founder-chairman of the Mauritius Union of Journalists, he led protests against the Newspapers and Periodicals Amendment Bill. Despite police refusing to arrest him due to his age, he walked to Line Barracks in solidarity with 43 arrested journalists.

🌿 Legacy

Beekrumsing Ramlallah passed away in 2000. In his honour, the Government of Mauritius renamed Mapou SSS in 2006. A Beekrumsing Ramlallah Interpretation Centre preserves his memory and contributions to Mauritius.

Curriculum & Examinations
Academic Structure
BRSSS follows the national curriculum framework set by the Ministry of Education, preparing students for internationally recognised Cambridge examinations at both SC and HSC level.
📚

Lower Secondary (Forms I–III)

Foundational education covering English, French, Mathematics, Sciences, History, Geography, ICT/Computer Science, Physical Education, Life Skills and Performing Arts across Grades 7 to 9.

🎓

Upper Secondary (Forms IV–V)

Preparation for the Cambridge School Certificate (SC) at Grade 11. Students study core subjects alongside elective pathways including Additional Mathematics, Design & Technology, Economics, Accounting and more.

🏆

Sixth Form (Forms VI–VI Upper)

Higher School Certificate (HSC) programme at Grades 12–13. Three major streams: Science, Commerce/Economics, and Arts/Humanities — each aligned with Cambridge A Level standards.

BRSSS nurtures well-rounded citizens through a range of extracurricular activities, community service initiatives and creative projects that complement academic excellence.

Former Students
Alumni Association

🎓 Beekrumsing Ramlallah SSS Alumni

The BRSSS Alumni Association was established to reconnect former students — affectionately known as "Ex-Mapou Boys" — and build a network that supports the school and future generations. The association's mission is to leverage the collective experience of former students to improve the school and contribute to the Mapou community.

The association organises yearly activities at school level, awareness campaigns and collaborative initiatives with current students and staff. Alumni are encouraged to share knowledge, mentor students and support sustainable development at BRSSS.
